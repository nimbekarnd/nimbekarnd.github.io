About Me

Robotics engineer passionate about intelligent autonomous systems

Who I Am

I'm a Robotics Software Engineer with over 3 years of experience developing production-grade autonomous systems. My work focuses on the intersection of perception, planning, and control—building robots that can navigate complex environments, make intelligent decisions, and work alongside humans safely and efficiently.

At Vecna Robotics, I've had the privilege of working on warehouse automation systems that operate in real-world, high-stakes environments. I've led development of features that reduced robot contention by 25%, improved detection accuracy to 95%, and cut facility integration time by 30%. These aren't just numbers—they represent hundreds of hours of on-site debugging, countless edge cases handled, and real impact on warehouse operations.

My technical foundation spans the full robotics stack: from low-level sensor processing (LiDAR, cameras, IMUs) to high-level task planning and multi-robot coordination. I'm comfortable diving deep into point cloud algorithms, tuning PID controllers, or architecting distributed systems with ROS and Docker.

What Drives Me

I'm fascinated by the challenge of making robots that work reliably in messy, unpredictable environments. Simulation is one thing, but building systems that handle real-world variability—bad lighting, sensor noise, humans behaving unexpectedly—that's where the interesting problems live.

I believe the future of robotics isn't just about individual robots getting smarter, but about fleets of robots that can coordinate, learn from each other, and gracefully handle failure. That's the direction I want to push the field.
